## Copper price today

<PriceTicker metal="copper" />

Copper trades on the **COMEX** (New York) and **LME** (London) futures exchanges, with the spot price updated continuously during trading hours. Yards reference one or both when setting their daily payout.

## Today's macro signals

The factors most-watched by traders right now:

- **LME warehouse inventory** — when stocks draw down, near-month prices rise
- **China construction PMI** — China is the largest copper consumer; weak data pulls prices down
- **EV production data** — EVs use 3–4x the copper of ICE vehicles
- **U.S. dollar index** — copper is dollar-denominated; weak dollar lifts copper
- **Refining margins** — when smelter capacity is tight, scrap discounts widen

## How yard pricing reflects spot

A typical bare-bright copper payout is **85–95% of COMEX spot**, with regional and grade adjustments:

- High-end yards in major metros: 92–95%
- Mid-range yards: 88–92%
- Smaller / rural yards: 80–88%

These ranges shift with market conditions. When copper is volatile, yards widen their spread to protect against intraday moves.

## What to do with this number

- **Time large sales around favorable spot moves** — but don't try to perfectly time the market
- **Confirm yard payout vs. headline spot** — the gap is the yard's margin
- **Lock in a quote when possible** — some yards will hold quoted pricing for 24 hours on confirmed loads
