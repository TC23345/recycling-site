## A universal search method

Every "near me" page on the site recommends the same three-step approach:

1. **Search "scrap yard" + your city** in Google Maps — yards with active listings surface
2. **Filter for recent reviews and photos** — abandoned listings are common in this industry

## Vetting any "near me" yard

A short universal checklist:

- **Current scale certification sticker** at the scale house
- **Visible posted prices** (yards that hide pricing usually have reasons)
- **Active operations** — trucks moving, a graded staging area
- **Reasonable wait times** at peak hours
- **Clean ID process** — they should ask for ID without making it weird
- **Reviews** — recent (last 12 months) and not all 1-star or 5-star

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the difference between a "scrap yard" and a "metal recycler"?**

In practice, very little. Some operators brand as "recyclers" to emphasize environmental positioning; others brand as "yards" to emphasize buying scale. Most do both. The directory pages above all index the same universe of operators.

**Can I just walk in?**

At most yards, yes — bring photo ID, expect a scale-in process, and plan for 20–60 minutes depending on queue. Larger commercial sellers usually have peddler accounts.

**Do near-me yards pay the same as the big national operators?**

Often yes for non-ferrous; sometimes less for ferrous (where mill-proximity matters more). Always call around — see [Selling Guide → Pricing](/selling-guide/pricing).
