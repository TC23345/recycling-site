## Precious Metals Guide

> **Page brief.** Target keyword: `precious metals guide`. Audience: people inheriting jewelry, scrapping electronics, sorting old coin collections, or pricing bullion. Funnel stage: awareness → consideration. The page should answer: *what are precious metals, how do their markets differ from industrial scrap, and where should I start?*

Gold, silver, platinum, and palladium trade in their own world — quoted in **troy ounces**, sold to **refiners and coin shops** rather than scrap yards, and priced off **LBMA fixings** rather than COMEX or LME spot. This silo covers the basics, the purity ladders, the refining economics, and the macro forces that move spot day-to-day.

## Key differences from industrial scrap

| | Industrial scrap (copper, aluminum) | Precious metals (gold, silver) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pricing unit | USD per pound | USD per troy ounce |
| Reference | COMEX / LME spot | LBMA fixing + spot |
| Payout factor | 60–95% (varies by grade) | 80–99% (varies by purity + buyer) |
| Buyer | Scrap yard | Coin shop, refiner, jeweler |
| Volume threshold | Small lots fine | Trips matter — over $5k worth, mail-in pays better |

## Frequently asked questions

**Is selling gold jewelry "scrap"?**

Sort of. Refiners call it "scrap gold" because they melt and re-refine it, but the economics are very different from scrap copper or aluminum. Refiners pay 80–98% of spot equivalent — a much tighter spread than scrap yards.

**What about platinum and palladium?**

Both trade actively but show up in scrap mostly via **catalytic converters**. The rest of the precious-metal volume on this site is gold and silver; we cover catalytic converter material in the [auto recycling guide](/recycling-guide/auto) instead.

**Where do I sell?**

For under $1,000 of material, a local coin shop usually pays within a few percent of mail-in refiners and lets you walk out with cash same day. For larger lots, mail-in refiners with insured shipping typically pay better. See the [refining & selling](/precious-metals-guide/refining) category for vetting tips.
