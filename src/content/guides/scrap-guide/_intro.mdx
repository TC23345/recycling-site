## The scrap guide: what it is, how it's graded, what it pays

> **Page brief.** Target keyword: `scrap guide`. Audience: scrappers and sellers who want a single map of the trade — from "what counts as scrap" to "what a yard actually pays for a stripped #1 copper load." Funnel stage: awareness. The page should answer: *what does a working scrapper actually need to know to walk into a yard and not get burned?*

This hub is the front door for the scrap silo. The four categories below split the trade into the questions sellers actually ask: what is this stuff, how is it graded, what tools earn their keep, and what happens at the yard. Use it as an index — every link points to a deeper page.

## What this hub covers

Most scrap content online is either copy-paste yard marketing or surface-level "recycle your cans" listicles. The pages under this hub are written for someone who has a load in the truck bed and wants to know what it's actually worth. Sections to expect:

- A working definition of scrap that distinguishes ferrous, non-ferrous, and the awkward middle (stainless, brass alloys, mixed-metal assemblies)
- Grade ladders — the shorthand that yards use on the price board (#1 copper, HMS 1, sheet vs. cast aluminum, 304 vs. 316 stainless)
- Equipment that pays for itself across a season of scrapping
- How a yard visit actually goes — the scale, the magnet, the haggling, the payout

## Topic ideas / outline

The pages under this hub will eventually cover:

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the difference between this guide and the recycling guide?**

This guide is for sellers — the person delivering a load. The [Recycling Guide](/recycling-guide) is the wider story: what happens to your scrap after the yard, plus auto, e-waste, and C&D streams that don't always run through a traditional scrap yard.

**Where do I start if I've never sold scrap before?**

[Scrap for Metal: A Beginner's Guide](/scrap-guide/basics/scrap-for-metal). Then read the [Scrap Yards](/scrap-guide/yards) category before your first drop-off so you know what to expect on the scale.

**What's worth more — a tidy small load or a messy big one?**

Tidy almost always wins per pound. Yards downgrade mixed loads to the lowest grade present. The [Scrap Grades](/scrap-guide/grades) category covers the math.
