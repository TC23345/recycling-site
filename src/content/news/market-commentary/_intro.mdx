## Market commentary

Daily and weekly read on the metals tape — what's driving copper, aluminum, ferrous, stainless, gold, and silver, and what to watch into the next session. The questions in focus are the practical ones: is now a good time to move material, what's the likely direction, and which grades feel the move first.

## What this category covers

- **Daily tape** — close-of-day commentary on big moves across copper, aluminum, ferrous, stainless, gold, and silver
- **Weekly recap** — Friday wrap-ups linking AISI mill utilization, AMM ferrous benchmarks, COMEX/LME settlements, and DXY moves
- **Cycle reads** — when the broader cycle is rolling over (or bottoming) and what that historically means for scrap payouts
- **Cross-metal context** — copper-aluminum correlations, nickel-stainless spreads, and gold-silver ratio moves

## Why market commentary matters

Per-pound payouts at a yard track exchange settlements with a lag — typically same-day for copper, next-business-day for aluminum, weekly for ferrous. Reading the tape helps time a delivery within that window. For the mechanical translation from exchange to yard, see [Industry Guide → Trade & Pricing](/industry-guide/trade-and-pricing).

## Frequently asked questions

**Are there trading recommendations here?**

No. Market Commentary is editorial — it explains what moved and why. For pricing decisions, confirm rates with a local yard and consult a licensed broker for futures.

**How does this differ from the live price page?**

The pricing pages (e.g. [Copper](/copper-price)) carry the current quote and a 30-day chart. Market Commentary explains the path the price took to get there and what's likely next.

**What's the data source?**

Live spot from Metals.dev (with Yahoo Finance as fallback) for industrial metals; LBMA fixings and Metals.dev for precious. Ferrous benchmarks reference AMM weekly. Macro data from the Fed, BLS, and AISI.
