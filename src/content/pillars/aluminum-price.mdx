## Aluminum grade ladder

| Grade | Description | Typical % of sheet |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sheet (clean) | Mill-grade, no paint or coating | 100% (reference) |
| Extrusion | Window frames, structural extrusion | 90–100% |
| Cast | Wheels, engine blocks, cookware | 70–85% |
| UBC (cans) | Used beverage cans, baled | 60–75% |
| Painted / coated | Painted siding, anodized | 50–70% |
| Mixed / unsorted | Anything that isn't separated | 40–60% |

## What moves aluminum prices day-to-day

- **Energy prices** — primary aluminum production is extremely energy-intensive; secondary (recycled) production gains favor when energy is expensive
- **Auto-sector demand** — aluminum is increasingly used in vehicle bodies and EV battery housings
- **Beverage can demand** — UBC pricing tracks consumer beverage volumes
- **Bauxite supply** — primary feedstock; supply disruptions push secondary up
- **LME warehouse stocks** — the most-watched inventory signal

## Selling tips

- **Clean sheet pays the most** — strip paint and labels where economic
- **Don't mix cast with sheet** — entire load defaults to cast pricing
- **Cans pay best in volume** — small UBC sales are barely worth the trip
- **Aluminum is non-magnetic** — easy to separate from steel with a magnet pass before loading
