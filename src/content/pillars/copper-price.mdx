## Pricing — the most-watched data on this site

Copper is the bellwether of the scrap world. It trades on the **COMEX** futures exchange in New York and the **LME** in London, and yards quote local pricing as a discount off whichever exchange spot they reference. Aluminum, brass, and stainless prices follow similar logic, though their reference markets differ.

## What moves these numbers

- **Macro demand** — copper rises with global construction and EV manufacturing
- **Inventory levels** — LME warehouse draws are watched closely
- **Currency** — a weaker dollar typically lifts USD-denominated metals
- **Energy prices** — refining cost feeds into ingot pricing
- **Local supply/demand** — your regional yard's pricing reflects how full their yard is and where their buyer is
