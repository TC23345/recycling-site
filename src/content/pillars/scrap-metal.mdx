## Scrap metal: from waste stream to ingot

Scrap metal recycling is one of the oldest and most efficient industrial loops in modern manufacturing. Roughly **two-thirds of the steel produced in North America** comes from recycled scrap, and aluminum recycling uses about **5% of the energy** required to make virgin aluminum from bauxite.

For sellers, the practical implication is that there's a real, liquid market for almost any clean metal — but pricing reflects how easy your material is for the mill to use.

## The price-volume-grade triangle

Three variables determine what a scrap load is worth: the **metal type** (governs the per-pound base), the **grade** (a discount or premium relative to base), and the **volume** (volumes change the math on transportation). The articles in this cluster work through each variable individually.
