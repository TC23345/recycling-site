## Finding a scrap yard you can trust

A "scrap yard near me" search returns dozens of options in most metro areas, and they're not interchangeable. Yards differ in what metals they accept, how they grade material, whether they have certified scales, what hours they operate, and — most importantly — what they actually pay.

This cluster gathers practical guidance for finding a reputable yard, calling around for quotes, and avoiding the common pitfalls that cost sellers money on the scale.

## What yards typically accept

Most full-service yards take:

- **Ferrous metals** — steel, iron, appliances (with refrigerants removed), structural and rebar
- **Non-ferrous metals** — copper, aluminum (sheet, cast, extrusion, cans), brass, lead, stainless
- **Cable and wire** — graded by gauge and insulation
- **Catalytic converters** — typically a separate counter; bring ID

Specialty yards may focus on auto bodies, e-waste, or precious metals. Always call ahead for unusual loads.
