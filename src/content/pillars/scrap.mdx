## What "scrap" actually means

In the trade, **scrap** is any metal recovered for recycling — whether it came off a job site, out of an old appliance, or from industrial production runoff. Scrap is sorted, graded, and sold by weight to mills and foundries that melt it back into new product.

The two big buckets are:

- **Ferrous scrap** — iron-bearing material like steel and cast iron. Cheaper per pound but moves in volume.
- **Non-ferrous scrap** — copper, aluminum, brass, lead, stainless. Higher per-pound payouts; the focus of most amateur and side-hustle scrappers.

Within each bucket, grade matters as much as metal. Bare bright copper pays roughly twice what insulated copper wire pays. #1 prepared steel pays more than unprepared sheet because it's already sized for the mill's furnace charge.
