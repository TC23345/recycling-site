## What this is

Whats My Scrap Worth? is a community resource for anyone who wants a clear answer about scrap metal pricing, recycling, and what their material is actually worth. Whether you're a household with a garage cleanout, a contractor pulling a tear-down, a yard operator setting daily prices, or someone selling a coin collection — the aim is an honest read on the trade without a sales pitch attached.

Live pricing for the metals most readers actually deal with (copper, aluminum, brass, stainless, gold, silver) sits alongside plain-English guides on grading, recycling, where it all goes, and what to expect at a yard. The goal is to demystify a market that has historically rewarded insiders.

## Who it's for

- **Households** — garage cleanouts, estate sales, the inherited box of "junk metal" from grandpa's shop
- **Scrappers and small operators** — daily benchmarks, grade reference, prep tips
- **Contractors and demolition crews** — rebar, copper wire, HVAC, structural steel
- **Yard operators and buyers** — a published baseline to point sellers to
- **Jewelry, coin, and bullion sellers** — what a refiner or coin shop will actually pay vs. spot
- **Journalists, analysts, and procurement teams** — a current read on the trade

## How the pricing works

Live pricing is sourced from public market data and refreshes roughly every minute. Standard scrap discounts are applied so the numbers reflect what a typical yard would pay, not the raw exchange spot. Local yards quote tighter or wider depending on grade, region, and how full their inventory is — treat these numbers as a baseline, not a quote.

All prices are **indicative** — they are not buy or sell quotes, and Whats My Scrap Worth? does not transact in metal.

## What this is not

Whats My Scrap Worth? is **not a buyer**. Not a yard, broker, or refiner. Not affiliated with OmniSource, Sims, Schnitzer/Radius, SA Recycling, Cohen, Weissman's, or any other recycler. Vendor profiles on this site are informational write-ups, not endorsements or partnerships.

No material is collected. No payouts issued. No shipments brokered. If a page profiles a vendor, that's editorial coverage — not the brand operating in the trade.
