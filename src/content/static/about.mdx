## What this is

Whats My Scrap Worth? is a community resource for anyone who wants a clear answer about scrap metal pricing, recycling, and what their material is actually worth. Whether you're a household with a garage cleanout, a contractor pulling a tear-down, a yard operator setting daily prices, or someone selling a coin collection — the aim is an honest read on the trade without a sales pitch attached.

Live pricing for the metals most readers actually deal with (copper, aluminum, brass, stainless, gold, silver) sits alongside plain-English guides on grading, recycling, where it all goes, and what to expect at a yard. The goal is to demystify a market that has historically rewarded insiders.

## Who it's for

- **Households** — garage cleanouts, estate sales, the inherited box of "junk metal" from grandpa's shop
- **Scrappers and small operators** — daily benchmarks, grade reference, prep tips
- **Contractors and demolition crews** — rebar, copper wire, HVAC, structural steel
- **Yard operators and buyers** — a published baseline to point sellers to
- **Jewelry, coin, and bullion sellers** — what a refiner or coin shop will actually pay vs. spot
- **Journalists, analysts, and procurement teams** — a current read on the trade

## How the live pricing works

Live pricing is fetched server-side and runs through a deterministic source chain so the provenance of every number is visible.

- **Copper** — primary source is [Metals.dev](https://metals.dev) when an API key is configured (LME-stamped spot, USD/lb directly). Falls back to Yahoo Finance's public chart endpoint for the COMEX `HG=F` futures contract.
- **Aluminum** — primary source is Metals.dev (USD/lb directly). Falls back to Yahoo Finance's `ALI=F` contract, converted from USD/metric-ton to USD/lb.
- **Brass** — derived from live copper × ~0.62, reflecting typical Cu/Zn alloy mass weighting. Inherits whichever provider was used for copper.
- **Stainless and prepared steel** — currently stub-priced. No free public ticker maps cleanly to scrap stainless; a dedicated nickel feed will replace the stub once the integration is justified.
- **Gold and silver** — primary source is Metals.dev (USD/troy-ounce, LBMA-fixed for gold).

Every payload is validated with [Zod](https://zod.dev). If the upstream shape ever changes — or Metals.dev hits a quota or auth error — the affected metal silently falls through to the next source in the chain (Metals.dev → Yahoo → deterministic-jitter stub). The active source is tagged on each snapshot (`source: "metals-dev" | "yahoo" | "derived" | "stub"`) so provenance is visible at all times.

### Discount factors

The numbers shown here are **scrap payouts**, not exchange spot. Standard discount factors map COMEX/LME spot into what a typical yard would pay:

- **Copper:** spot × 0.92
- **Aluminum:** spot × 0.70

Local yards quote tighter or wider depending on grade, region, and how full their inventory is. Treat these numbers as a baseline, not a quote.

### Refresh cadence

- Server-side fetches are edge-cached for ~60 seconds.
- The browser polls the `/api/prices` route every ~30 seconds and flashes any value that changed.

All prices are **indicative** — they are not buy or sell quotes, and Whats My Scrap Worth? does not transact in metal.

## What this is not

Whats My Scrap Worth? is **not a buyer**. Not a yard, broker, or refiner. Not affiliated with OmniSource, Sims, Schnitzer/Radius, SA Recycling, Cohen, Weissman's, or any other recycler. Vendor profiles on this site are informational write-ups, not endorsements or partnerships.

No material is collected. No payouts issued. No shipments brokered. If a page profiles a vendor, that's editorial coverage — not the brand operating in the trade.

## Sources & disclaimers

- **Pricing data:** [Metals.dev](https://metals.dev) `/v1/latest` (primary, when API key is set) and Yahoo Finance public chart endpoint (`HG=F`, `ALI=F`) as fallback. No warranty of accuracy. Yahoo can change or remove its public endpoints at any time; Metals.dev's free tier is rate-limited so caching is aggressive.
- **Editorial:** written and maintained as the underlying market or grading conventions change. Sources are linked when a price level or regulatory deadline is referenced.
- **Always confirm with your local yard.** Live numbers here are reference points. Before loading a truck, call ahead — yards quote net of grade, contamination, and current inventory.
